    Assignment

    • Save example pictures for low, medium and high Shutter Speeds in your picture library. Make sure to try really high and really low values. Give the files meaningful names.
    • Document your findings in your portfolio (especially very high vs. low shutter speeds). What are your results? How could you use the effects you see to your advantage for picture compositions and different motives?

    Introduction

    Shutter Speed is one of the most interesting elements of photography for me. It can be used in a huge number of ways. For example, if we need to make the water freeze, or take a picture of the night sky.

    I took some interesting pictures which you can see below.

    For the night sky shot, I used a very low shutter speed (20 minutes). Since my camera did not allow me to set this parameter to more than 30 seconds, I used the phone's camera and a tripod. I also rode a bike a little further from the city, as there is usually a huge amount of light in the city, which makes it difficult to take such pictures. Of course, it’s ideal to go to the mountains far from civilization, but I didn’t have such an opportunity.

    The next 2 photos were taken with medium shutter speed (approximately 0.5 seconds). This is enough to get the blur effect, but still not enough for the whole photo to turn into a blurry incomprehensible thing.

    Conclusion

    As a result, we can conclude that shutter speed gives photographers more freedom in how they can show a photo. If we take as an example a photo of a waterfall with a low and high shutter speed, then in the first case we get a photo that shows the flow of water, and in the second, attention is focused on individual drops and details. An interesting element with a huge field for usage.
