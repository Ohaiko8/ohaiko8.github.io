    • Background Compression

    Assignment

    • Take many pictures of the same subject with different focal lengths, while moving away from the subject and then zooming in again so that the subject takes roughly the same space in the frame.
    • What can you observe? How can this be useful? Where would you use that effect?
    • Add your pictures and a description of your findings to your Portfolio.

    Introduction

    When changing the focal length and approaching or moving away from the object, we can observe an interesting effect.

    When using a wide view lens, we can observe that a lot more elements are placed in the background behind the object compared to a narrow view lens. It seems to me that this knowledge can be wonderfully used in portrait photography. For example, if we need to depict a person behind whom there is a beautiful landscape that we also want to focus on, then we can use a wide view lens and approach the person (but do not take too wide a lens, as the proportions will be distorted). As a result, we get a beautiful portrait of a person behind whom is the same beautiful view.

    We can also use a narrow view lens to get rid of the clutter of the background with unnecessary details. In general, this effect has a lot of use cases in photography.

    Conclusion

    In conclusion, I want to say that this effect is simply mandatory for mastering if you are interested in portrait photography. Because the ability to show the background correctly is no less important than the ability to beautifully depict a person. And this effect can help us with this difficult task.
